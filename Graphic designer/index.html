Job Description

As a Graphic Designer with a focus on Advertisement and Posters, your primary responsibility will be to create eye-catching, innovative designs that capture the attention of the audience and communicate the intended message effectively. This role involves developing and executing design concepts for various advertisement campaigns, including print and digital media.

Job Resposiblities

  • Concept Development

    Develop creative ideas and concepts, choosing the appropriate media and style to meet the client's objectives.

  • Design Execution

    Create visual aspects of marketing materials, websites, and other media, including infographics, posters, and advertisements. Utilize various graphic design tools and software (such as Adobe Creative Suite) to produce high-quality designs.

  • Brand Consistency

    Ensure that all designs adhere to the brand's guidelines and convey the brand's style and tone effectively.

  • Collaboration

    Work closely with the marketing team, copywriters, and other designers to ensure a cohesive visual narrative across all channels and campaigns.

  • Market Research

    Keep up-to-date with emerging design trends and technologies, particularly in the field of advertisement and posters.

  • Project Management

    Manage multiple design projects from concept through completion, ensuring all work is delivered on time and within budget.

Creative Skills

Creativity

Graphic designers always come up with new and unique ideas to grab people’s attention and get their messages across in the best possible way. They use their creativity to make outstanding designs.

Design Principles

Graphic designers skillfully use elements like lines, color, shape, typography, scale, dominance, emphasis, and harmony to create visually appealing and well-structured designs, making sure everything looks eye-catching and balanced.

Branding

When working on branding for clients, graphic designers start by understanding what makes a brand special, and then they use logos, colors, typography, illustration, photography, and graphic elements to make the brand even better and more unique.

Typography

To make powerful visual communication with text, graphic designers need to be good at choosing the right fonts and using techniques like typesetting, kerning, tracking, and leading to make the text look picture-perfect.

Technical Skills

Designing for Print

Even with the rise of digital media, graphic designers must learn print design principles like bleeds, crop marks, ink limits, and paper sizes, as well as file formats and color systems, to make sure their print projects look fantastic on paper.

User Experience (UX) and User Interface (UI) Design

When graphic designers understand the basics of UX and UI, they can make designs that look great and work well, making the user experience better. It also helps them work smoothly with UX and UI designers on the team.

Technology

Graphic designers need to become experts in design software like Adobe Illustrator, InDesign, Photoshop, After Effects, and Sketch. It can also be helpful to know programming languages like HTML and CSS and understand content management systems like WordPress for their work.

Portfolio

For graphic designers, having a portfolio is a must. It's a showcase of their best work that proves how creative and skilled they are. Game animators can use an animation or demo reel in their portfolio to show off their skills and styles to potential employers.
